# The Call - A Dramatic Monologue
# Uses: author_neutral, author_excited, author_sad, author_angry
# A story of one man's emotional journey through an unexpected phone call

[author_neutral] It started like any other Tuesday morning. Coffee in hand,
emails piling up, the usual office noise humming in the background.
I wasn't expecting anything special.

[pause 0.5s]

[author_neutral] Then my phone rang. Unknown number. Normally I'd ignore it,
but something made me pick up.

[pause 0.3s]

[author_excited] They said yes! After three months of interviews, after all
those sleepless nights preparing, they actually said yes! The dream job at
the company I've been following since college. I couldn't believe it!

[pause 0.3s]

[author_excited] My hands were shaking as I wrote down the details. Senior
position, corner office, the whole package! I wanted to run through the
hallways shouting the news to everyone!

[pause 0.5s]

[author_neutral] But then she asked me to hold for a moment. Said there was
one small detail to discuss. The line went quiet.

[pause 0.8s]

[author_sad] The position... was in Tokyo. They needed someone to relocate
within two weeks. Two weeks. How could I leave everything behind so quickly?
My family, my friends, the life I've built here over the past ten years.

[pause 0.3s]

[author_sad] I thought about my mother's face when I'd tell her. About missing
my niece's graduation. About the promises I made to people who counted on me
being here. The excitement drained away, replaced by this heavy weight in my
chest.

[pause 0.5s]

[author_angry] And then it hit me. They knew. They knew about the relocation
from the very first interview! Three months of my time, my energy, my hope,
and they never once mentioned I'd have to leave the country!

[pause 0.3s]

[author_angry] How could they string me along like that? Did they think I'd
just abandon my entire life on two weeks notice? That I'd be so desperate
for their fancy title that nothing else would matter?

[pause 0.5s]

[author_neutral] I took a deep breath. Then another. The woman came back on
the line, waiting for my answer.

[pause 0.3s]

[author_sad] Part of me wanted to say yes anyway. This opportunity might
never come again. Dreams don't knock twice, that's what my father always said.

[pause 0.3s]

[author_angry] But another part of me refused to reward their games. If they
wanted me badly enough to wait three months, they could wait a little longer
for a reasonable timeline.

[pause 0.5s]

[author_neutral] So I told her I needed the weekend to think. She agreed,
surprisingly. Maybe they're more flexible than they let on. Maybe there's
still a way to make this work.

[pause 0.3s]

[author_excited] Because here's the thing, despite everything, they chose me!
Out of hundreds of candidates, they picked me for the role I've been dreaming
about since I was twenty-two years old!

[pause 0.3s]

[author_neutral] Life rarely offers perfect choices. Most of the time, we're
choosing between different kinds of difficult. The question isn't whether
it will be hard. The question is whether it will be worth it.

[pause 0.5s]

[author_excited] And you know what? I think it might be. I really think it
might be. Tokyo, here I come. Eventually.

[pause 1s]
